Jalapeno Cream Cheese Wings

My Favorite Wings

When I first moved to Korea way back when, I noticed there was a lack of good chicken wings restaurants around Seoul. Don't get my wrong, Korea has some of the best fried chicken in the world, but when it came to classic American style chicken wings they were no where to be found!

This all changed one day when my South African co-worker told me about a restaurant in Itaewon called Philies. He said, "Bro, these are exactly like the American style wings you've been talking about!" Not only that, but every Thursday they had a special going on 10 wings for 5000 won! 5000 won is equivalent to about 4 USD!

Needless to say I became a regular when it came to wing Thursday. Beers and delicious wings for cheap, you can't beat it! While I was there I came across a new flavor of wing, that my coworker swore by. Jalapeno cream cheese. I was a traditional hot wing and lemon pepper guy, but when I tried these wings, I became instantly hooked!

I am back in America now and I can't seem to find these wings at any of the local places. So lucky for you, I found the recipe online! Please do try this recipe out, I guarantee you won't regret it!

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken wings
  • Salt and pepper

Sauces

  • 2 slices of bacon, cut small
  • 1 tbsp. butter
  • 1 tbsp. all-purpose flour
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 3 tbsp. cream cheese
  • 1/8 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1 jalapeno, halved, seeded, minced
  • 1/2 tbsp. coarse whole grain mustard
  • Pinch of ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp. paprika

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 450F. Line baking sheet with a Cooking Sheet. Add chicken wings; season with salt and pepper. Place chicken so it is skin side down first.
  2. Bake in oven for 20 minutes. Flip, and bake for another 20 minutes.
  3. Place medium-sized saucepan over medium heat. Add bacon and cook for a few minutes. Stir in butter. When melted, add garlic, jalapeno, and flour. Cook, stirring constantly, for 5-7 minutes, or until the roux has browned in color.
  4. Add in milk, cream cheese, mustard, cumin, and parpika. Whisk, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil. Stir in cheddar cheese and cook until desired thickness is achieved. Remove from heat.
  5. Toss sauce with chicken wings OR place sauce on the side to dip your chicken wings into.
